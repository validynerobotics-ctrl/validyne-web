Engineering Depth

WHAT WE'RE
CAPABLE OF

Our engineering spans mechanical design, power electronics, real-time control, and systems integration — delivered as a unified capability across every programme we take on.

01

Autonomous Navigation

Full-stack autonomous navigation for mobile robots operating in structured and unstructured environments. Mapping, localisation, real-time path planning, and dynamic obstacle avoidance — indoor, outdoor, or mixed terrain.

02

Robotic Systems Design

Ground-up mechanical and systems design for special purpose robots. Chassis, drive systems, structural engineering, and full system integration — built for the demands of real field deployment, not lab conditions.

03

Perception & Intelligence

Computer vision, sensor fusion, and environment understanding for robots that need to see and interpret the world around them. Object detection, pose estimation, gesture recognition, and scene understanding applied to real robotic tasks.

04

Embedded & Control Systems

Real-time firmware, embedded control loops, and safety-critical software for robotic platforms. From bare-metal motor control to full navigation stacks — written and owned entirely by us, not outsourced or assembled from vendor SDKs.

Engineering at Scale

COMPLEXITY WE HAVE
BUILT BEFORE

Our team has designed and delivered systems at a level of complexity that most robotics companies don't attempt. The examples below give a sense of the engineering depth we bring to every programme.

01

Heavy Payload AMR — Up to 10 Tons

Autonomous mobile robots engineered for extreme payload capacities in demanding industrial environments. Custom drive systems, fail-safe braking, and navigation architectures designed around the physics of moving heavy loads safely.

02

Outdoor Autonomous Navigation — 3-Ton Payload

Heavy-duty outdoor AMR systems operating on uncontrolled terrain with shared vehicle traffic. Purpose-engineered navigation, environmental ruggedisation, and safety systems for conditions that standard AMRs are not designed to handle.

03

Autonomous Spray Painting System

A mobile base with a 4-metre vertical gantry and mounted robotic arm for industrial painting and coating. Dual SLAM architecture — one for object mapping, one for base navigation. Custom path planning algorithms, surface selection interface, and automatic calculation of paint volume, nozzle parameters, and hardening schedules based on user-defined coating specifications.

04

Vision-Based Gesture Control

Markerless, wearable-free gesture control of robotic arms using computer vision. The operator controls the robot through natural hand gestures — no gloves, no markers, no additional hardware required.

05

Robotic Bin Picking

Vision-guided robotic arm systems for unstructured bin picking. Handles variable part geometry, random orientation, and cluttered bins — the kind of pick-and-place problem that defeats rigid fixture-based automation.

06

Multi-System Integration

Complex robotic systems combining multiple subsystems — mobile bases, custom perception, path planning, and real-time control — engineered and integrated as a single cohesive system, not assembled from separate vendor components.
